## Public showcase (the drop-in content)

### Hero
Headline (recommended): **Built, not bought.**
Subhead: "The Hurricane Bath moves up to about ten gallons a minute, the kind of flow you get from
city water, and dials all the way down to a soft trickle for a dog's face and eyes. Paul built it
himself, and it gets a dog genuinely clean, fast."

### Why he built it
None of the systems Paul could buy did everything he wanted in one machine. So he built the one
that does. Years of working with the professional systems showed him what to keep and what was
missing, and he put all of it into a single machine tuned exactly the way he wants to wash a dog.
There is nothing else quite like it, and he is still refining it.

### Power, dialed to the dog
A lever right at the nozzle sets the water anywhere from a soft trickle to full force. Full force
drives water through a thick double coat all the way to the skin, which is where a real clean
happens. A soft setting is right for a face, eyes, and sensitive spots. No fire-hosing where it
does not belong. Powerful does not have to mean rough.

### Your dog finishes on clean water
The wash recirculates, and it gets a dog clean the same way a dishwasher does: the water moves
through the system over and over, and what comes out is clean. Every dog finishes the same way,
with a fresh-water rinse from a separate tank.

### No dog too dirty
A dog caked head to toe in mud is not a problem. When the wash water turns to a mud puddle, Paul
flushes the whole system, refills it with clean water, and washes again, as many times as it
takes, until that dog is washing up like an already-clean dog. There is very little a coat can
hold that the Hurricane Bath cannot get out.

### Shampoo that suits your dog
Clean washes with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that sits well with nearly every dog, the kind
of thing no one has reason to complain about. If your dog does better on a specific product, bring
it. It loads into the system before the bath even starts, so your bottle is back in your hands
before your dog is wet. Medicated or prescription shampoo from your vet works the same way: bring
it and we use it. Flea shampoo too, though it is worth knowing that no single bath solves fleas,
since a dog picks them right back up at home or in the yard. For that, your vet's plan is what
fixes it, and we are glad to use whatever they recommend.

### Easy on water
Most dogs are washed and rinsed with under five gallons. The power comes from how the system moves
water, not from wasting it.

### Fast
The system is quick. A faster bath is a calmer, shorter visit for your dog, and that speed is what
makes washing a very dirty dog twice over no trouble at all.

### The experience
Powerful does not mean rough. The pressure dials down to a soft trickle for a face or a nervous
dog, the trailer stays the calm, familiar place, and the dog comes out genuinely clean.

### The name (optional, light touch)
The name leans into Florida: a hurricane washes everything clean. Use only if it adds something;
no big origin story needed.

### FAQ (draft)
- **Is it gentle?** Yes. The pressure dials down to a soft trickle, so faces, eyes, and small or
  nervous dogs get a soft bath.
- **Is my dog washed in clean water?** The wash recirculates and gets your dog clean the way a
  dishwasher does, an extra-dirty dog is flushed and washed again with fresh water, and every dog
  finishes on a clean-water rinse from a separate tank.
- **What shampoo do you use?** A gentle shampoo and conditioner that suits nearly every dog. If
  your dog needs something specific, bring it and we will use it.
- **My dog needs a medicated or flea shampoo.** Bring it and we will use it. One note on fleas: no
  single bath solves them, since a dog gets re-exposed at home, so your vet's plan is what fixes
  the problem.
- **How dirty is too dirty?** Just about nothing. Mud from nose to tail is a normal day.
