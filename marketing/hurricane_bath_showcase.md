### What it is (client-facing)
The Hurricane Bath is a wash system Paul designed and built for the trailer. He took the best of
the professional bathing systems, left their compromises behind, and tuned the result over years.
It delivers up to about ten gallons a minute, and a lever right at the nozzle dials that power
from almost nothing up to full, so the bath is as gentle or as strong as the dog and the coat
need.

### Why it is better
- **Power you can control.** On a thick double coat, blasting high pressure at a dry dog just
  sprays water everywhere. Paul dials the pressure down to soak the dog first, then turns it up to
  drive water through the undercoat all the way to the skin. That is where a deep clean comes
  from, and a setup that cannot change its pressure cannot do it.
- **The cleanest bath he knows of.** Far cleaner than washing by hand, and cleaner than any
  mobile setup he has used.
- **Gentle on water.** Most dogs are washed and rinsed with under five gallons, which matters in
  a trailer and is easy on the planet.
- **Your shampoo, no problem.** If your dog needs a specific product, Paul drops it straight into
  the system and hands the bottle back. Any shampoo works.
- **A clean machine for every dog.** The system is built to come apart and get cleaned, and Paul
  rinses it down between baths. Your dog is not bathed in the last dog's water.

### The story (why he built it)
The professional bathing systems each did one thing well and one thing wrong. The old Hydrosurge
recirculated water to save it, but the pump sat in the tub with the dog. The Bathmaster kept the
pump out of the way but mixed shampoo into a fresh-water flow, which a trailer with a limited tank
cannot afford. The built-in RV systems that came later recirculated with a separate pump, but the
whole thing lived under the tub where it was a nightmare to keep clean. Paul took the best of all
of them and dropped the compromises: the power of a pro system, the water thrift of recirculation,
the pump kept away from the dog, and a design he can pull out and scrub so it is spotless for the
next dog. There is nothing else exactly like it.

## Open questions to make it more awesome
1. **Water temperature.** How is the water heated or kept warm? Warm water is something clients
   care about a lot, and it is not in the showcase yet.
2. **The final rinse.** Does the dog finish on fresh water from the rinse hose, not recirculated
   water? This preempts the biggest unspoken worry ("is my dog washed in dirty water?").
3. **The name.** Why "Hurricane Bath"? A good origin for the name is a strong hook, and you own
   hurricanebath.com.
4. **The result clients notice.** Does the deep clean show up as less shedding, less dander or
   smell, relief for itchy skin or hot spots? Concrete results sell harder than "cleaner."
5. **Speed.** Does the power make the bath faster, which means a calmer, shorter appointment?
