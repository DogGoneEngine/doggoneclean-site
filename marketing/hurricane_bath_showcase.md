## The moat decision (read first)

**Sell the what, protect the how.** The only truly copyable part is the parts list: the exact
pumps, their power, and the plumbing. Everything else (the result, Paul's judgment, years of
refinement, the cleaning discipline) cannot be copied off a web page even if described in full,
which is why it is the moat. So be generous with the benefits and the story; keep only the build
recipe private (it lives in CLEAN_FIELD_MANUAL.md, "The Hurricane Bath").

## Public showcase (the drop-in content)

### Hero
Headline (recommended): **Built, not bought.**
Alternates: "The most powerful bath in the field." / "City water pressure, in a trailer."

Subhead (draft): "The Hurricane Bath moves up to about ten gallons a minute, the kind of flow you
get from city water, and dials all the way down to a soft trickle for a dog's face and eyes. Paul
built it himself, and it gets a dog genuinely clean, fast."

### What it is (client-facing)
The Hurricane Bath is a wash system Paul designed and built for the trailer. He spent years with
the professional bathing systems, kept what worked, and built one machine that does everything he
wants. It delivers up to about ten gallons a minute, and a lever right at the nozzle dials that
power from a soft trickle up to full, so the bath is exactly as gentle or as strong as the dog
and the coat need.

### Why it is better
- **Power, dialed to the dog.** Full force drives water through a thick double coat all the way to
  the skin. A soft setting is right for a face, eyes, and sensitive spots. No fire-hosing where it
  does not belong.
- **Finishes on clean water.** The dog is washed in the recirculating system, then rinsed with
  fresh water from a separate tank.
- **Clean even while recirculating, like a dishwasher.** A dishwasher reuses its water and your
  dishes still come out clean. Same idea here. And if a dog is extra dirty, Paul flushes the
  system, fills it with clean water, and washes again, sometimes twice over, until the dog is
  washing up like an already-clean dog.
- **Fast.** Speed is a big part of its strength. A faster bath is a calmer, shorter appointment,
  and it is what makes washing a very dirty dog twice over practical.
- **Your shampoo, no problem.** Any product you provide drops straight into the system; Paul
  washes with it and hands the bottle back.
- **A clean machine for every dog.** The system comes apart and gets cleaned, and Paul rinses it
  down between baths.

### The story (why he built it)
Paul did not buy the Hurricane Bath. He built it. After years of working with the professional
bathing systems, he took everything he liked, added what was missing, and put it into one machine
tuned exactly the way he wants to wash a dog. There is nothing else quite like it, and he has been
refining it ever since.

### The experience (dog and client)
Powerful does not mean rough. The pressure dials down to a soft trickle for a face or a nervous
dog, the trailer stays the calm, familiar place, and the dog comes out genuinely clean.

### The name (optional, light touch)
The name leans into Florida: a hurricane washes everything clean. That is about the size of it.
Use only if it adds something; no big origin story is needed.

### FAQ (draft)
- **Is it gentle?** Yes. The pressure dials down to a soft trickle, so faces, eyes, and small or
  nervous dogs get a soft bath.
- **Is my dog washed in clean water?** The wash recirculates and gets the dog clean the way a
  dishwasher does, an extra-dirty dog is flushed and washed again with fresh water, and every dog
  finishes on a clean-water rinse from a separate tank.
- **Can you use my shampoo?** Yes. Any product you provide goes straight into the system.
